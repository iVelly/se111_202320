REVIEW of the movie Scott Pilgrim vs. The World

Levi Germeil

February 13, 2023

★★★★★

"Scott Pilgrim vs. The World" is a movie that has it all. From the hilarious and quick-witted dialogue to the unique visual style and amazing cinematography, this movie is a must-see for anyone who loves movies that are both fun and thought-provoking. As a teenager who loves movies, I found "Scott Pilgrim vs. The World" to be a perfect fit for my interests.

The film's humor is top-notch, and had me laughing out loud from start to finish. But what really sets this movie apart is its underlying messages and themes. The story of Scott Pilgrim's journey to win Ramona's heart is not only entertaining, but it also explores deeper themes of personal growth, relationships, and the consequences of our actions. The character development of Scott Pilgrim and the other characters around him is well-written and authentic, making the emotional payoff of the story all the more satisfying.

In addition to its great storytelling, the movie's unique visual style is nothing short of breathtaking. Edgar Wright's use of split-screens, transitions, and other visual effects kept me engaged and mesmerized throughout the entire film. I especially loved the incorporation of elements of video games and comic books into the movie's visual style, making it a truly unique and immersive viewing experience.

Watching "Scott Pilgrim vs. The World" has inspired me to be more creative with my own storytelling and to think outside the box when it comes to visual techniques. Edgar Wright's ability to use cinematography to grab the viewer's attention and add depth to the story has shown me just how powerful this tool can be in filmmaking.

Overall, I highly recommend "Scott Pilgrim vs. The World" to anyone who loves great cinematography, innovative storytelling, and a mix of humor and deeper themes. It's a movie that can be enjoyed by both teenagers and adults alike, and I can't wait to watch it again!

Scott Pilgrim vs. The World Movie Trivia

  • At the party where he talks to Ramona for the first time, Scott holds up a very badly drawn picture of Ramona and asks if anyone has seen her. Director Edgar Wright said Scott was originally supposed to hold up a picture of Ramona from the comic book series, but felt the silly drawing Scott holds up was more comedic.
  • When Scott and Ramona are riding the bus, the background lights are distorted into hearts when Scott is onscreen. They're initially distorted into Xs when Ramona is onscreen, but switch to hearts about half-way through the scene.


  • Each time Ramona's hair and clothes change color so does Scott's color scheme. When he first meets her at Julie's party her hair is pink and her shirt is pink with blue accent, he's standing next to her wearing pink t-shirt beneath a blue outer shirt. The night she shows up at the apartment with blue hair and blue shirt under her coat, shortly after, when they go meet Wallace at Lucas Lee's shoot, Scott is standing next to her again wearing a blue inner jacket and later when they are walking down the street together with coffee her outer coat is blue and so is his. Then when she's been taken by Gideon and her hair and shirt are green, when Scott suits up to rescue her he's wearing a green shirt with green and white wristbands.
